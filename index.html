The Outreachy Sprint: Friday, March 20th

It was around 5:00 PM in Lagos, Nigeria. It was a Friday, and I was deeply engrossed in a personal project. But honestly, my mind was restless. I had been anticipating the Outreachy qualification email all week. According to the timeline, if you didn't qualify for the next phase, you wouldn't get an email, you know the feeling. I found myself checking my inbox every few minutes, battling impatience and hoping for the best.

And then, it popped up. At first, it felt unreal. Finding out I had officially qualified for the contribution phase brought a rush of pure excitement. But almost immediately, anxiety settled in alongside it. "What's next?" I wondered, especially as I needed to select a project. That evening marked the beginning of an intense, challenging, and incredibly rewarding journey into the Fedora community.

The Fedora Family: A Challenge That Wasn't

Entering the Fedora Project with little prior open-source experience felt intimidating at first. I felt a bit out of my depth. But I quickly realized that Fedora's "Four Foundations" aren't just a mantra—the community truly lives by them. It is incredibly welcoming, and everyone is genuinely ready to hold your hand.

I felt immediately integrated into the family, as if I had been there for years. I was free to ask questions and received answers that actually helped. My mentors and the other contributors were always available to explain things. Before long, as I received help, I found myself helping others too. The technical challenges I anticipated faded away because I knew I was never stuck, whenever I had a question, someone was there. My initial interest in AI and ML brought me to the project, but the people made me fall in love with Fedora.

Unexpected Discoveries: Becoming "Superman"

I've always been someone who refuses to label anything "unaccomplishable." I doubled down on that strength during this short sprint. Almost all the tasks and projects I worked on were relatively new to me. I came in thinking I could code and that I was a very good technical writer, but I learned so much more.

I became adept at advanced Markdown, mastered Docusaurus formatting, and aligned with rigorous Fedora writing guidelines. My problem-solving and collaboration skills skyrocketed because I chose to take on any task, knowing the community had my back. Exploring Docling, testing RamaLama models, and running deep analytical comparisons, something I hadn't done since high school, sharpened my research skills profoundly. In this short space of time, overcoming these steep learning curves made me feel like Superman. I was making new discoveries every single day, collaborating effortlessly, and making genuine friends.

The Takeaway

Looking back, I believe this contribution phase has perfectly set me up for the actual internship. I now feel like a true part of the community family. I have been nurtured by the foundations and tested by the work. Having navigated all these hurdles during the contribution phase, I know I can comfortably take on any task or project during the internship. It can only get better from here!
